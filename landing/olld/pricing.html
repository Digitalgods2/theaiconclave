Frequently asked

The honest answers.

Q01Do you mark up API tokens?
No. You bring your own keys and pay the model providers directly at their rates. The AI Conclave only charges for the chamber infrastructure: orchestration, dissent engine, audit recording, exports, and project memory.
Q02What happens to my data?
It stays on your machine. The AI Conclave runs locally. Your prompts, transcripts, and decision records never touch our servers on Solo or Keeper plans. Chamber plan customers can choose self-hosted or our hosted infrastructure — never both default.
Q03Can I run this entirely on open-weight models?
Yes. Set up OpenRouter, point the chamber at it, and you can run a full session without ever touching a frontier provider. The tool-loop reading feature means open-weight seats stay cost-effective even on long contexts.
Q04Why is Solo free forever?
Because the value compounds the more you use it. Project memory, decision archives, and the Charter all get sharper over time. Solo gives you a real working chamber. Keeper is where it becomes a daily tool. We'd rather have you on Solo for a year than churn off a trial.
Q05Is there a free trial of Keeper?
Fourteen days, no card. If you don't ratify at least one verdict during the trial you've probably picked the wrong tool — refund without questions.
Q06What does "Charter-customizable" actually mean?
The chamber operates under an internal constitution that governs seat roles, dissent handling, ratification rules, and audit requirements. Keeper users can amend it within bounds. Chamber users can ratify their own. The system that governs decisions is itself governed.
Q07Will you ever turn this into a SaaS that holds my data?
No. Local-first, single-user is a load-bearing design decision. If we change it, it won't be The AI Conclave anymore.
