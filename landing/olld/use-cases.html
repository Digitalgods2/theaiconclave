Where the chamber earns its keep

When wrong is expensive.

The AI Conclave is built for decisions that depend on judgment but punish you for being wrong. The features stop being "cool" and start being practical risk reduction the moment the cost of failure shows up on a balance sheet, a security report, or a courtroom transcript.

▸ DOMAINS All Engineering Security Legal Finance Medicine Product Research Ops Policy
01
▸ SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

Code review before it ships.

refactors arch deps debugging

Probably the strongest fit. Codex, Claude Code, and Gemini already live in the engineering workflow. The chamber promotes them from "tab I check" to "council I consult." Architecture choices, dependency upgrades, refactors that touch shared modules, security tradeoffs in critical paths.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"We're replacing our auth library. Three candidates. Which one survives a five-year horizon and what do we lose with each?"
02
▸ CYBERSECURITY

The miss that costs millions.

incident triage threat model patch risk

Preserved dissent matters most here. One model missing an indicator is the difference between a contained incident and a public breach. The chamber records exactly who flagged what and when, so the post-incident review writes itself.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Suspicious outbound traffic from a build agent at 03:14 UTC. Triage. Is this exfiltration or a misconfigured probe?"
03
▸ LEGAL & COMPLIANCE

Not advice. A second read.

contracts policy regulatory discovery

Not a replacement for counsel. A structured review pass that catches what one reader missed. Contract redlines, policy interpretations, regulatory readings, discovery summaries. The audit trail is the deliverable: courts and regulators want to know what was considered, not just what was decided.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Vendor agreement, third draft. Indemnification clause moved. What changed materially since v2 and what are we accepting?"
04
▸ MEDICAL & RESEARCH SUPPORT

Second opinions, traceable.

literature protocol insurance summary

Not diagnosis. The chamber is for the administrative and research layer around clinical work: literature review, protocol critique, insurance documentation, summarizing treatment options for patient discussion. The value here is traceable reasoning a clinician can actually defend.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Five recent papers on this protocol. Summarize methodology, flag the weakest study, identify the assumption all five share."
05
▸ FINANCE & INVESTMENT

Test the thesis. Stress the assumption.

research risk due diligence stress test

Investment memos forget why they chose what they chose. The chamber preserves the assumption set alongside the conclusion. Comparing models, surfacing blind spots, stress-testing a thesis against a deliberately skeptical seat. When the position moves against you twelve months later, the chamber tells you whether you missed it or accepted it.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Long thesis on this name assumes 18% top-line growth and stable margins. Which assumption breaks first and what kills it?"
06
▸ PRODUCT MANAGEMENT

When every option is defensible.

prioritization roadmap build vs buy pricing

Product is dense with defensible answers. Build vs buy. Ship now vs polish. Free tier vs paywall. The chamber gives you a record of what you considered and why you rejected the alternatives — useful for the all-hands two quarters later when someone asks "why didn't we just buy it?"

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Customer feedback is split 60/40 on this feature. Ship as-is, ship gated, or kill it? Argue all three positions."
07
▸ SCIENTIFIC RESEARCH

Consensus can hide weakness.

paper review methodology grant stats

Peer review is good. A council of frontier models with adversarial roles is a different angle on the same problem. Methodology critique, experimental design pressure-testing, statistical sanity checks, grant proposal review. Dissent is what you want here.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Draft of section 4. Power analysis assumes effect size from the 2019 paper. Is that assumption defensible given the 2023 replication?"
08
▸ DEVOPS & PLATFORM

Decide. Deploy. Don't regret.

deploy postmortem migration backup

The deliberate-decide-execute-record loop fits operations almost too cleanly. Deployment reviews. Outage postmortems. Config changes touching shared infrastructure. Backup and restore plans. The chamber's transcript becomes the runbook annotation you'll wish you had at 2am.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Postgres major version upgrade, prod. Rolling vs maintenance window vs blue-green. Trade-offs, failure modes, rollback path."
09
▸ MANAGEMENT & POLICY

The decision behind the decision.

hiring vendor policy process

Hiring rubrics. Internal policy drafts. Vendor selection. Process changes that touch how the team operates. The chamber lets you show what was considered and why something was rejected — which is most of what good management documentation actually is.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"Remote-work policy revision. Three drafts. Which one survives contact with a quickly-growing team?"
10
▸ TECHNICAL WRITING

Edit pass with an opinion.

editing fact-check audience fit structure

One model gives you suggestions. A chamber gives you a contested edit pass: this paragraph is weak, no it's load-bearing, the structure is fine, no it buries the lede. Editorial dissent is sharper than editorial consensus.

▸ EXAMPLE SESSION
"This piece runs in a developer publication. Section 3 is the part I'm least sure of. Cut, rewrite, or leave it?"
